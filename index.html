How the World Spends Its Day

Follow the daylight around the globe and compare how people in different parts of the world spend their 24 hours.

Daily Time Use Comparison

Sydney, Australia

The story begins in Australia as the first place the sun greets as each day begins. In Sydney, the average person spends a significant portion of their day sleeping, followed by work and leisure activities.

Tokyo, Japan

In Japan, time is divided differently. Sleep takes up less time while work, paid and unpaid, takes up more than in Australia. The pattern of daily life is already starting to look different...

Delhi, India

South Asia brings another pattern of daily life shaped by its own social and economic patterns. Compared to Japan, there is less time devoted to leisure and more time devoted to unpaid work, which includes household chores and caregiving.

Paris, France

Europe enters the scene in the form of France, where the time for leisure is more than in the previous two countries, and the time for paid work is the least of all countries we have explored. The average person in France spends as much time sleeping as in Sydney, much higher than Delhi and Tokyo.

Cape Town, South Africa

Africa adds another perspective. South Africa shows a mixed distribution of time, where care, work, and leisure all compete for space in the day. Still, leisure occupies the least amount of time of the explored countries, similar to India.

Mexico City, Mexico

The final stop brings the Americas into view in the form of New Mexico. The pattern of time use in Mexico is similar to that of South Africa, with a more even distribution of time across different activities. New Mexico has the least amount of time devoted to leisure, comparable to India and South Africa. The amount of time devoted to sleep is average as compared to the other countries, more than in Delhi and Tokyo but less than in Sydney and Paris.
