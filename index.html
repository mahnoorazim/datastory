How the World Spends Its Day

Follow the daylight around the globe and compare how people in different parts of the world spend their 24 hours.

Daily Time Use Comparison

Australia

The story begins in Australia, where a large share of the day goes to sleep and personal care. Paid work is the next major block, but leisure still holds a meaningful portion of the day, creating a relatively balanced profile compared with the countries that follow.

Japan

In Japan, the schedule tightens. Sleep is lower than in Australia, while paid work and travel take a larger share, reflecting a more compressed workday rhythm. The result is a day that feels more work-centered, with less flexibility around rest and downtime.

India

India brings a stronger unpaid-work burden into focus. Time spent on household tasks and caregiving is comparatively high, which reduces room for leisure. This shift shows how responsibilities outside formal paid work can still dominate daily time budgets.

France

France shows a different pattern, with more leisure and comparatively lower paid work time than several other countries in this story. Sleep also remains high, producing a profile that appears less time-pressured and more weighted toward recovery and personal life.

South Africa

South Africa presents a mixed distribution where paid work, unpaid work, and care all compete for space in the same day. No single category dominates strongly, but leisure remains more limited than in high-leisure profiles, highlighting tradeoffs across essential activities.

Mexico

Mexico closes the story with high unpaid work and relatively constrained leisure. Sleep stays near the middle of this country set, but the distribution shows how caregiving and household labor can absorb large blocks of the day even when paid work is moderate.
